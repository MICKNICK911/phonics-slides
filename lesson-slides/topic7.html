🔒 Closed Syllable

A syllable ending with a consonant, making the vowel short.

📖 Examples: cat, sit, mag-net

👂 Sound: Short vowels like /ă/ in "cat"

🔓 Open Syllable

Ends with a vowel that says its long sound.

📖 Examples: go, she, mu-sic

👂 Sound: Long vowels like /ō/ in "go"

✨ Magic E

Silent 'e' makes the previous vowel long.

📖 Examples: cake, note, theme

👂 Sound: Vowel + silent e like /ā/ in "cake"

🦁 R-Controlled

Vowel + 'r' changes the vowel sound.

📖 Examples: car, bird, fern

👂 Sound: /ar/ in "car", /er/ in "her"

👥 Vowel Team

Two vowels working together.

📖 Examples: boat, team, cloud

👂 Sound: /ō/ in "boat", /ow/ in "cloud"

🔠 Consonant-le

Appears at the end of words.

📖 Examples: ta-ble, bub-ble, lit-tle

👂 Sound: /əl/ in "table"

🎭 Schwa

Unstressed "uh" sound in multisyllabic words.

📖 Examples: so-fa, pen-cil, ba-nana

👂 Sound: /ə/ like "a" in "sofa"
